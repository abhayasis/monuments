Monuments of Odisha

Jagannath Temple: The Jagannath Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Jagannath, a form of Vishnu, located in Puri. The temple, which was originally built in the 10th century, is a major pilgrimage destination.

Learn More

Konark Sun Temple: The Konark Sun Temple is a 13th-century Sun temple at Konark, about 35 kilometers from Puri. It is designed as a massive chariot with elaborately carved stone wheels, pillars, and walls.

Learn More

Lingaraja Temple: Lingaraja Temple is a Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva and is one of the oldest temples in Bhubaneswar, Odisha. The temple represents the quintessence of the Kalinga architecture and culminates the medieval stages of the architectural tradition.

Learn More

Mukteshvara Temple: The Mukteshvara Temple is a 10th-century Hindu temple dedicated to Shiva located in Bhubaneswar. The temple is famous for its detailed sculptural work and its arched gateway, which is unique in Odishan architecture.

Learn More

Dhauli Shanti Stupa: Dhauli Shanti Stupa is a peace pagoda located in Dhauli hills, about 8 km south of Bhubaneswar. The pagoda was built in 1972 and symbolizes the transformation of Emperor Ashoka after the Kalinga War.

Learn More

Rajarani Temple: The Rajarani Temple is an 11th-century Hindu temple located in Bhubaneswar. The temple is known for its stunning architecture and is often referred to as a 'love temple' due to the erotic carvings of women and couples on its walls.
